# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Game and UI developers, technical artists, and future maintainers who need to revisit project decisions, implementation notes, and production standards. They arrive in a focused reading mode, often looking for a specific answer while still wanting the archive to feel personal and cared for.

## Product Purpose

This static blog turns project experience, code analysis, art-resource standards, and workflow notes into a durable reading archive. Success means the homepage makes the writing easy to scan, article pages feel comfortable for long technical reading, and the visual system gives the site a memorable personal identity.

## Brand Personality

Precise, atmospheric, crafted. The blog should feel like an engineer's studio wall: practical notes, visual texture, and a strong point of view without sacrificing clarity.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS-card layouts, washed-out gray technical documentation, neon developer clichés, purple-blue gradients, decorative glassmorphism, and template-like icon grids. The site should not look like a default documentation theme or an AI-generated landing page.

## Design Principles

- Make the archive feel authored, not auto-generated.
- Let typography and spacing carry the artistic tone before adding effects.
- Keep technical content readable under real working conditions.
- Use imagery as an editorial signal, not as filler.
- Keep interactions quiet, quick, and predictable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast for body text and controls. Preserve keyboard focus visibility, support reduced motion, keep line lengths comfortable for long-form reading, and avoid conveying meaning through color alone.
